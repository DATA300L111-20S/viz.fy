Welcome to viz.fy! Visualizations on the Spotify's daily top 200 tracks in the United States. You can view today's top tracks/download the data set here.

Music is a huge part of the world's culture, and in the digital age it seems almost random what will become a top song. We thought to explore today's top music and see if we could make any sense of today's most praised music.

We wanted to take a look at the shape of the 'Streams Curve" by plotting streams against top 200 position. This would give us a look into how drastically having a song in a higher position would increase a songs exposures / interaction.

Clicking any of the "filter buttons" in the top left will filter the data by the Top 200, Top 100, and Top 50.

Now lets look at the top 200's most decorated artists. A "top artist" for viz.fy, is any artist with more than one song on the top 200 list. The more songs an Artist has on the top 200 its safe to say the more likely they will continute to appear on future top 200 lists. This bar chart captures these top artists and how many songs they have on today's top 200 list.

Clicking any of the bars will take you to spotify and play you that artists top song, making it easy for you to discover new music.

Lastly we wanted to present what is present in top songs, through Spotify's audio features. We plotted the song's audio feature's value versus the song's top 200 position. Also we included a line of best fit so we could possibly idenfity any trends. If a trend exists its more likely that there's a correlation between a the value of the audio feature and the song's position.

Click the "cycle audio feature" button to cycle the audio feature we are visualizing.
